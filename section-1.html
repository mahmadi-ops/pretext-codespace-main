    Section 2 Infinite Series

    This section is about infinite series. We will mainly focus on presenting the definitions and providing some examples such as the geometric series.
    🔗

    Subsection 2.1 Basic Definitions

    Definition 2.1.

    A sequence is a list of numbers \(a_0, a_1, a_2, \ldots\) indexed by the natural numbers.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection 2.2 Partial Sums

    Definition 2.2.

    The sum of the first \(n \) terms in a sequence is denoted by \(S_n \) and it is known as a partial sum.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 2.3.

    Find the sum of the geometric series \(1 + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} + \cdots\)
    🔗
    Solution.
    Since this is a geometric series with first term \(a = 1\) and ratio \(r = \frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) the sum is:
    \begin{equation*} S_n =\sum_{i=0}^n a_i=\frac{a}{1-r} = \frac{1}{1-\frac{1}{2}} = 2 \end{equation*}
